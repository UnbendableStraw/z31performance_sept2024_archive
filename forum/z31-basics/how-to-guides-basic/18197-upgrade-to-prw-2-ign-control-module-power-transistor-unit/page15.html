    actually, 3rd party manufacturer/supplier Airtex had the idea before anyone else, not Nissan. don't be fooled by the sale ad or the part number, the 22020-12P11 number listed is the original nissan number and is merely there for compatibility verification (notice how it says "FITS" as the first word).

    the photo posted is what you get when you order the unit from Airtex; Part Number # 6H1213. Roughly $100. For all intents and purposes, I'd say the upgrade is somewhat worth 100 dollars if there were no way to achieve the same results by spending about 1/5th of that. But you get a fancy bracket, though! lol.




    if you use the supplied number and order it from Nissan, you'll get this:



    When nissan runs out of the transistors, they will be NLA or a new number will be issued. The old number gets you the old part (for restoration purposes, mainly).

    If you look closely, you can see the 12P11 nissan part number engraving on the baseplate/heatsink mount on the lower photo, too.
