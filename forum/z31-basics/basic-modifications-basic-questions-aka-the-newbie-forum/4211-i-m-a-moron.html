    I am going to help you out here for a second.

    So 420cc/m-480cc/m. You need to convert to lb/hr, You do this by dividing by 10.5. So 40-46 lb/hr injectors.

    Now for the fuel required for a 3 Liter, 2 Valve engine w/ say a Volumetric efficiency of 85%, spinning 6500 RPM @ 15-20 lbs of boost. Pressure Ratio is 14.7+Boost pressure Divided by 14.7.

    183(CID) x 6500(RPM) x .85 (VE) x 2.36 (PR)
    '''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''3 456''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''''' '''''''' = 690.4 CFM

    So, now we know how much air is flowing @ 20 lbs of boost we need the fuel to match it. Which is found by CFM x .44298 Divided by # of cylinders.

    690 x .44298
    '''''''''''''''''''''6'''''''''''' = 50 lb/hr injectors.

    50 lb/hr injectors. Remember these are rated at 43 psi with the injector operated at 100% duty cycle (Not pulsing)

    So you need more fuel. Not to mention a 9:1 Static compression engine @ 20 PSI has a Effective compression ratio of 21:1. So your little ECU idea will probably not fly so well.

    Now that you were GIVEN some basic formulas, you can figure out what you need from here. Hopefully this will help not look so lame to others. Good luck :-)
