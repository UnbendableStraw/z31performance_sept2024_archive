    quick look at the oil pan for the return line there (but that can be put on a NA motor easy)

    safety blow-off valve on front of intake manifold for turbos and it is blocked off for turbo (again, an easy switch)

    Yank heads and see if you have dished pistons. Otherwise, pretty much same bare motors.

    If it is in the car just look at the vin. If it is out of the car (which I am assuming it is) then you can do a compression test. I don't remember exactly what the different comps are on turbo vs. NA, but some searching should yield some results. Other than that… Look to see if it has an oil return line in the driver side of the oil pan. should be something like 1/2" or so… hose barb fitting coming right off the side of the oil pan. You can look to see if the oil supply line is plugged or if it is open from someone pulling the turbo and just leaving the hole there.

    There is probably others but that should get you going.
