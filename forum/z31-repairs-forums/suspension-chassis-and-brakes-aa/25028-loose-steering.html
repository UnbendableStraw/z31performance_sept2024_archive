    Hello
    I have an 87 Z31 I just replaced all "front and rear" shocks/struts with tokico blues "which I hate but its all I could afford". Replaced all bushings with poly including subframe, Ball joints, Inner Tie Rods, New PS rack and hoses with Derlin Right side and Poly L side mounts and it is solid ,Rack doesn't move at all. I have checked and rechecked all poly connections they are tightened correctly and nothing moves but here is what is going on…

    When going down the road the upon hitting some uneven parts of the road bumps or dips the wheel that hit the bump or dip pulls that direction and feels kind of loose i guess you would say it is tracking with the road. That would indicate play,,, But from where? I do have a little slack in my steering column "Very Little" maybe like a 1/4 in. So I do know that I will need to replace the steering knuckle with the little U joints in it. but would that translate into that much play at the bottom? Or could it be something else? I guess I will go ahead and replace that steering knuckle first and see if that helps.

    Does anyone have any suggestions on what to check next if that is not the case?

    Check the steering column universals. Both my 85T and sister's 85NA went bad back in the 90's. One way to tell if there is excessive steering play is to stand outside the car and move the steering wheel back and forth through out its on center play and watch the front tires. The amount the steering wheel moves before the tire moves is the play in the steering column or possibly steering rack.
    Restore it, Don't crush it. They don't make them like this anymore.

    Aha! I did the test as FlyingT suggested, way more play at the bottom as he suspected. So I replace my steering knuckle. Yes my baby u-joints were toast and wow what a difference. "Do they break?" and allowing them to jiggle around and give play. I am just going to replace the whole strut mount/bushing with new ones. The old ones are just that "OLD".
