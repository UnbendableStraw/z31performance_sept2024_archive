    Yeah, this was obviously too much extra thread… I had disassembled everything, then collected the bolts I thought were for the calipers when I went to paint them. I determined which bolts were the pin bolts and which were the hard mounts just based on diameter and pitch, not length… so when I put the strut and steering knuckle together, that extra thread just puzzled me.

    I guess after having worked on the engine and the suspension over the last several months, it's really amazing how few situations there are like this to get two bolts of same diameter but different lengths reversed. Every other time I found multiple lengths of the same diameter, there would be a different number of the "short" bolt than of the "long" bolt, so you could figure it out. Also, not many cases where the bolt hole is threaded all the way through the piece so one that's too long would work. I tried to "bag and tag" everything as I took it apart… but this was one I obviously missed.
