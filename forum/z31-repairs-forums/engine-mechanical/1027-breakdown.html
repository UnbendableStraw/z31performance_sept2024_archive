    Your phone call sparked my curiousity....(I wasn't up to full speed with what the issues are that you're having)
    *Are you sure it's the fuel pump?
    ~A few things to keep in mind:
    The fuel pump is part of circuit that involves being controlled by the ecu, which is controlling the relay. The ecu can under certian circumstances, lower the voltage to the pump to keep it quieter when fuel is under low demand.
    I have seen only 1 instance personally where the trace in the ecu burned apart. My resolve was to just hardwire the relay the ecu was controlling.

    You also mention in one of your posts hearing a sound coming from the same general area that the fuel pump relay is located.....
    This makes me believe that there is a possibility that it isn't the fuel pump, that it may be the ecu or the relay itself.
    If you take a peak at the Engine Management section on this site, there's a Sticky with directions to help you further diagnose your problem rather than just throwing money and parts at it and hoping foir the best. If it was in my shop…I'd be eliminating the relay and ecu as the culprit before doing what you are thinking.
