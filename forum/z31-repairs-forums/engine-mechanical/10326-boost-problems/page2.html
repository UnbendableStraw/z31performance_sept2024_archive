    When under boost, pressure on the valve and pressure on the diaphragm are about the same. The spring keeps it shut, but it doesn't take a lot of spring pressure to do this.

    When under no boost, there's little to no vacuum against the valve and a lot of vacuum on the diaphragm (after the throttle body). The spring's main purpose is to keep it shut here.

    But, after releasing the throttle from boosting, the spring has to be weak enough to allow enough boost to escape.

    So, you're still right. But not because of boost. It might be leaking under vacuum. If the BOV is setup for X psi correctly, it's setup for Y psi correctly. Most of the time.
