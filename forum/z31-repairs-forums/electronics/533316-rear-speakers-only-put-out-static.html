    The stock speaker system is weird in that it shares the chassis ground with the audio ground…iirc.

    Some possible ideas:

    Check for bad ground points causing noise in the system.

    Using the stock stereo, it very well could have gone out. I've rescued several from the junkyard in the past, looking for a good one only to find
    it doesn't put out sound, but lights up and finds radio signals. 30yr old electronics have issues, from my limited knowledge,
    it's usually faulty capacitors in the radio but, idk which ones. There are a lot.

    Using an aftermarket stereo and the stock wires, try running new wires to the speakers from the head unit and do not use the stock harness
    for anything but steady 12V and switched 12V supply. This silences bitching Betty on the front left speaker, but you could put a small speaker under the dash
    to the left of the drivers column where there is a cavity and wire the speaker to the stock left door speaker wiring to hear her lovely voice again.

    Using an aftermarket head unit, wires and speakers…see #1
