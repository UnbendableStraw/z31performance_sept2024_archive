    If the starter motor was shorted, I don't think that jumping the solenoid on the starter would allow the engine to turn over. I agree with 88sinZ, use the EL section to test starter magnetic switch and check wire connections. A bad connection can read correct voltage, but not be able to support the amperage draw required to activate the mag switch. I would check the "B" connection to the battery because if you were able to jump the "B" to "M" and get motor to spin, the problem is probably there. However, if you jumped "S" to "B" and it spun then its a problem with the switch circuit from the ignition. Take your time and check everything methodically, you WILL find the issue. Best troubleshooting advice I ever got was never assume the last guy who was in there did it right, especially if that guy was you!
    Good luck and keep us posted!
