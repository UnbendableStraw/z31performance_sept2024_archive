    I swung by my local junk yard and pulled mirrors from a J30 Maxima, '97 Infiniti J30 (I saw two different styles, could be trim dependent), and an Infinti Q45 (FY33 model). The Maxima rear view mirror looked basically identical to an S13 mirror I found. The Maxima mirror bolts right in to the Z31 location and comes with a plastic cover to make it look clean. However, for the life of me, I could not find a black mirror - just a muted gray color. I looked in at least 10 different J30 Maximas and did not find any black trim, so i think the color options are medium gray and tan/beige.

    Here's an interesting thing, both the Infinti J30 and Q45 mirrors will fit in the Z31, but not great. I attempted to bolt both of them up to a junked Z31 I came across. The J30 appears to have at least 2 different styles of rear view mirrors (one with auto dim and the other plain). I do not think either J30 version would work well in the Z31 due to the width of the mounting base and the fact that the plastic cover for the base is not integrated into the base itself, as it is on the Maxima and Q45.

    Now the Q45 mirror is an auto-dim version identical to the Infiniti J30, but the mounting base is slightly narrower and the stalk is shorter than the J30 and the base is flush with the stalk. This means if you wanted to mount the Q45 mirror to a Z31, you would likely need to cut a plastic/metal spacer to let the stalk clear the windshield bump in the Z31. Oh, and you would need to wire the mirror if you wanted it to work. I could see this working if someone really wanted a modern-style rear view mirror in their Z31 and was willing to put in some effort to engineer a good solution.

    I bought the Maxima/S13 mirror for $5 and it's almost perfect. I even have the rubber stopper that rests on the windshield Will need to be trimmed to fit in the Z31.

    In case anyone wonders what the J30 Maxima mirror looks like.

    Pros: Very stable. Doesn't shake at all at speed like my OEM one did. Integrated cover looks slick.

    Cons: The mirror sits closer to the windshield (higher up) and is taller than stock, so the viewing angle needs to be adjusted. You see more of the top of the rear hatch, so it might not work well for super tall people.
