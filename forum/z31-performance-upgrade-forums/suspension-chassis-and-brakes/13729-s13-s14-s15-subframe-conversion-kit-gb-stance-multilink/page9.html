    YOU WILL BE WELDING THINGS :tomates Thought that was clear from the beginning. This is a weld together kit, requires a lot of welding and cutting. Requires an experienced welder/fabricator for the install, though greatly simplifies install because all the parts are pre-fabbed. You will be cutting and welding and test fitting. You shouldn't need to be measuring or centering the subframe or aligning it.

    Why do the rear studs not come welded?
    The reason is simple, and I have already stated it. The fit between the studs and subframe must be a tight slip fit for strength. Variations in subframe and Z31 chassis mean the studs cannot be prewelded to the template as initially considered during prototype phases. The front tubes do not come pre-welded to the U-shape connectors for the same reason. Pre-welding means you will either have a sloppy fit, no fit at all, or be cutting everything apart and starting over.

    To me, the impact of this change is minimal.. Maybe 5 extra minutes and 1 extra process step. Here is an example:
    1.) Insert studs into subframe
    2.) Lay template/rear bar over studs
    3.) Weld to studs (AS PICTURED)
    4.) Remove rear bar with studs attached from subframe. Bam, its now a perfect fit to YOUR subframe.

    If your worried about your welding skills causing things to warp, maybe its not for you. I'm not a pro welder by any means, but as you can see, I easily welded everything together without warping or distortion. Also remember, this stuff isnt 18ga sheet metal. Its thick, strong stuff.
