    Sorry Guys

    Ive been kinda dead on the forums for a while.

    so as it turns out fitting a 280zx Booster was a total bitch for the following reasons.

    1) the bolt pattern is way different so you need to build a an offset spacer block with studs pointing out so it can be bolted into the Z31 holes.
    2) the BMC doesn't fit unless you slot the holes or use a 280zx one no big deal.
    3) it definitely is shorter in length but its actually quite a lot wider than a Z31 Booster.


    Advantages
    1) its rated and sized for a 15/16th Master as a Z31 rolled off the production line with.
    2) the early 280ZX boosters have those handy angle cut outs which give you additional clearance to objects like custom manifolds or exhausts
    3) even with an adapter block its quite a lot shorter than a Z31 booster.
    4) its still from a ZX so if you have asshole inspectors like in NZ you can just say you bought a replacement from a ZX parts guy.
