    There is not much in the way of aftermarket for those but it's pretty common on these cars.
    They used a flex hose behind the motor, then essentially made it solid again with the thick bolted on heatshield.

    It can be welded, if the person is experienced welding cast materials. I have had one of mine welded over 10 years ago and it's held up.
    I installed hardware on the heatshield that would allow it to float and not tie the two sides together solidly.

    I'm also planning to weld mine. I've researched this over the last year, and have found all OEM and all aftermarket caste iron manifolds cracking. Even new motor mounts didn't help. Plus, the Dorman brand and all that available junk out there don't build their product to fit correctly. Some people (on Xterra and Frontier) are having to grind off corners to get them to clear accessories on installation. Also, the aftermarket heat shields suck. Poor fitment. I'd say weld the OEM. Best fit for sure and possibly stronger (not tested yet). I would also be careful about the surface mounting to the heads. If the average Joe machine shop attempts to shave these for flatness, don't let them do it without a geometrical reference point to keeping the new flat surface in alignment with the rest of the system. This will certainly cause them to crack again.
