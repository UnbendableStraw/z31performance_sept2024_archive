    The easiest way to control the vgt is with a wastegate actuator. At low boost the vgt vanes stay closed to around .55 AR. As soon as boost reaches that of the wastegate spring the vgt opens and increases the AR to somewhere right under 2.00. Yes of course you have to run an external wastegate. VGT has nothing to do with regular wastegate function.

    By wastegate function i mean diversion of exhaust gas around the turbo, which the vgt's do not do. They control boost by lowering the aspect ratio of the hotside. This is fine for a diesel but on most gasoline motors the lowest boost people can get them to hold on the he351ve by just closing the vanes was around 25psi. On most gasoline motors, the vgt works best when used be to gain faster spool instead of boost control. This makes it necessary to add an external gate.
