    cooper plates. They never used MLS systems in GTP. Cooper plates were designed to withstand extreme amounts of boost 45+PSI. These plates were receiver and bead lock grooved to fit like a puzzle to the deck of both the heads and block. They were made of billet aluminum and were .095" thick.

    Jesel made the rocker arms years ago and have since lost all the R&D specs on the assemblies.

    The V52 heads were completely reworked with a Chevy valve guide and valve combo. The valves were sodium filled for a stronger yet slighter valve assembly. Chambers were left primarily stock with the exception of over sized seats and deshrouding of the exhaust/intake areas then the areas were blended and radiused. The intake floors were filled with epoxy to generate a different valve/port angle. This not only increased tq/hp but it also aided in a better line of site and significantly increased air speed.
