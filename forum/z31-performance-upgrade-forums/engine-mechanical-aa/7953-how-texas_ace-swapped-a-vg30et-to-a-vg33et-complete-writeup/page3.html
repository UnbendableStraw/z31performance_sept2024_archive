    I agree, how you've done it is definitly better, and it's how I would have like to do it, now that I found the oil pump for cheap and all that… but I didn't have the time to pull the Z31 motor, so…

    As for the paint… I do impress people with my painting skills out of a spray can sometimes. I have a different approach. Depending on the shape of the parts, sometimes it works, sometimes it doesnt. But regardless, I always get it to come out super shiney.

    I used Duplicolor engine enamel for the gold parts (they look slightly vibrant, but I promise they're more subdued and more like the electromotive gold used on the GTP ZX Turbo engines and Infiniti M30 engines).

    I spray really thick coats until you can see the mist turn into a sheen. I don't spray from like 20 inches away and mist it until it speckles the entire part over and over… all you'll get doing that is speckling of the paint and it will end up flat. Every time I spray until it levels itself out into a glossy finish, I stop and let it dry. Then I do the same with the clear.

    The clear is weird. It's actually white when it's applied in excess… but if you can apply it in excess and not have it run, and it turns the part white, when it dries it will look like GLASS.

    Racinjitter saw my valve covers last year. asked me what I painted them with. LOL. They've since been repainted, due to me having to take off the intake manifold a gazillion times and getting frustrated.

    One thing I will note though, is that using the engine clear creates a "soft" layer on the paint. Easily scratched, and easily stained. But at that point, you can use a scotch brite pad to break it down to a flat version of whatever colour is underneath, and then respray if you please.
