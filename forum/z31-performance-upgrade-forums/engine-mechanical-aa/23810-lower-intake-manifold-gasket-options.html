    Well I had the vac line blow off my fpr on the dyno on a high boost run before winter and it seems to have damaged my piston rings and head gasket so I got to pull my engine apart yet again sigh.

    I am looking into what parts to get for this I figure a engine gasket set is pointless with the modifications to the engine since I have the intake spacer so thats just rtv silicone, vg33 heads and such so I am planning on going with ishino head gaskets, late model pathfinder vg33 stamped metal exhaust manifold gaskets from nissan, the custom piston rings from BCR performance for the custom 8.5:1 cr pistons, so not much else is needed that comes with a gasket kit.

    The big thing I am wondering about is the lower intake gaskets. Looking at Nissan the stock 88 nissan gasket part number 14035-Z31001 is $31.32 this is metal with those rubber rings around the passages. When I was looking at the vg33 pathfinder for the exhaust manifold I saw the lower intake gasket was a stamped metal one with no rubber rings around the passages part number 14035-R50002 for $6.41. Is their any problem with using the cheaper pathfinder gasket? I know it will work on the engine, but is the quality better, the same, or worse? Can it withstand boost? I know they had superchargers on the pathfinders but they didn't run alot of boost. I am running high boost on the car I was making around 435hp at the wheels when I had the vacuum line failure on the dyno.
