    Re: This is what it sounds like when T-5's die.

    Yes, input shaft normally snaps first in all the Z's T5 failures I've seen.

    1SickZ wrote: Fix the input shaft all you want:

    I have a 30A counter-shaft input gear that looks just like that....

    but as 1sickz seems to have been noting here… I'll summarize. The T5 is the lightest manual trans for the Z31 (though almost the same weight as the NA manual 71C). It has a relatively small case and small internal gears by comparison to the 30A. I have a picture somewhere of a T5 countershaft next to a 30A countershaft. The 30A gear teeth are at least 30% wider, so if it were made of the same material taking the same force (which it's probably not, but just for the sake of rough comparison), it should be about 30% stronger. I will post this picture when I find it.

    Now by simply comparing the available aftermarket gearsets, the T5 would by far     be the transmission to build should you want to spend the time and money to do it. You don't really need a kit, as the parts car be ordered… just all the research Russ has done to get the right combination for the Z's T5 is the "valuable information".
