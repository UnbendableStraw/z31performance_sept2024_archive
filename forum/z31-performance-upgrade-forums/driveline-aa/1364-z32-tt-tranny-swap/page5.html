    I found a good write up with pics of a Z32/RB25 hybrid tranny.

    First pics show a Z32 tranny being taken apart.

    http://www.rbz32.com/viewtopic.php?f=2&t=57



    To just swap the bell housings all you need to do is remove the throwout bearing, and pull the pivot fork off the pivot ball, slide it out of the bell housing.

    Also pull the outter detent ball out of the trans. Remove the 17mm bolt on the side of the transmission and remove the spring and then remove the ball. Keep these in a safe place.


    Then remove the 10mm bolts that hold the front cover on, the front cover will pull off with some force (its gasketed on)

    There will be one large snap ring, and one small snap ring, remove both (you'll see them)

    Now youll need to remove all of the 14mm bolts in the center of the trans (these hold the bell housing on)

    Now grab your mallet and whack the bell housing till its loose, it'll slide right off

    Reverse the process (dont forget new gaskets or RTV) and you've got a new hybrid trans

    its very easy.
    the hardest thing is to remove the check ball retainer (not the check ball itself).

    you must remove the check ball retainer to pull the bell housing off. i tried for an hour to hammer the shit out of the bellhousing to get it off because we couldn't remove the retainer. once we managed to get the retainer out the bell housing came out like a bawseeeeeeee.

    really it's simple.

    remove all side bolts that hold the bell housing on.
    remove the seal plate inside the bell housing.
    remove the c-clips and snap rings that hold the shaft and the shift rod in place.
    do not remove the locating pins in the rear of the transmission shifter housing… it's unnecessary unless removing the back half of the case (no need for this type of job!)
    remove the check ball and spring, then the check ball retainer.

    bell housing comes off.
    do the same to the z32.
    and swap bell housings.

    it's best to press out the counter shaft bearing in the bell housing and put a new one in, since you're there.
    also, you should get a new seal for the cover plate, and new c-clips/snap rings if you damage yours taking them out (like if you don't have a set of snap-ring pliers and use something else to get them out like I did).

    and you'll need a new paper gasket for the seal plate. some light brushing of RTV to seal the case halves together is ideal. and as for the speedometer sensor- i think there is one from a pathfinder that works, though I'm not sure yet.

    I am going to replace the check-ball retainer on my trans unless the one on the Z32 fits and it's in good condition. there is an o-ring that will need replacing so maybe that will do it.

    I believe its 32 dollars and it's covered in the RB26/Z32 trans swap on HybridZ by Boost3d.
