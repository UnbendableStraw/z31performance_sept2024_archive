    It reportedly has additives that erode some of the elements used in older nissan transmission blocker rings, eventually causing synchronizer failure. I've heard this from numerous sources so far, and combined with the number of Nissan manual transmissions that have gear grinds with very few miles on them I figure it might be good to get the word out.     I've been told that Redline and all other synthetic oils are GL-5 certified without the purposed harmful shock additives, meaning they are safe for transmissions you should otherwise not use GL-5 in.
