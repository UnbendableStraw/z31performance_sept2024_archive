    Take the dizzy out (but leave the harness connected/reconnect it), disconnect the injectors. It's much easier to see if you are getting an RPM signal by turning it by hand. You can get 2-300 rpms easily. Much easier on your battery.

    Seems like it's not connected to the right signal line. You should get something while cranking if it's hooked up correctly, even if your grounds are bad. Also verify that you get ~12v on the signal line, works best with a analog meter. There is a pullup resister, right?
